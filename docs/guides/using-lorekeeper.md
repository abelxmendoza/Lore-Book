# Using Lorekeeper

Lorekeeper is not a chatbot. It's a system that gradually builds a record of your life through conversation — and uses that record to respond more accurately over time.

This guide explains what to expect, how to use it well, and what it actually does with what you share.

---

## The Core Idea

Every time you send a message, two things happen:

1. You get a response
2. Lorekeeper quietly processes what you shared — extracting people, places, recurring themes, decisions, events — and adds it to your record

The more you use it, the more accurate and personal it becomes. Not because it's getting smarter, but because it knows more about you.

---

## What Kinds of Things to Share

Lorekeeper handles any kind of message, but works best when you share things that actually happened or matter to you:

**Things that build your record:**
- "I had a hard conversation with my sister today"
- "I've been working at the same company for three years and I'm starting to feel stuck"
- "My mom called again. Same situation as before."
- "I finally decided to move to Portland"

**Things that don't build a record (but still work fine):**
- Questions ("how do I write a cron job?")
- Hypotheticals ("what if I quit my job?")
- Roleplay or fictional scenarios

Lorekeeper classifies every message automatically. If it's a real experience you're sharing, it goes into your record. If it's a question or thought experiment, it responds without storing it.

---

## What Lorekeeper Remembers

After you share something, Lorekeeper extracts and remembers:

- **People** — Anyone you mention regularly gets tracked as a character. "My mom," "Abuela," "my coworker James" all become named entities in your record with their own history.
- **Events** — Things that happened with a beginning and end. A trip, a job change, a difficult conversation.
- **Recurring themes** — If you mention the same situation across multiple sessions, Lorekeeper starts recognizing it as a pattern: a recurring relationship, an ongoing challenge, a developing story.
- **Decisions** — Major choices you made. These become part of your timeline.

---

## How Continuity Works

Continuity accumulates gradually. After your first conversation, Lorekeeper knows very little about you. After ten conversations over a few weeks, it starts to recognize recurring people and situations. After months, it has a sparse but genuine record of what your life looks like.

"Sparse" is intentional. Lorekeeper doesn't fabricate rich context it doesn't have. It only asserts things it has actually seen you share. When it doesn't know something, it says so — and invites you to share it rather than pretending.

**Signs continuity is working:**
- Thread entity chips appear next to conversation titles (the small tags showing recurring people/topics)
- In a new conversation, Lorekeeper references something from a previous one without being told
- When you mention someone you've talked about before, the response reflects that history

---

## Threads as Revisitable Records

Each conversation thread is a permanent record. When you return to a thread, you return to exactly where you left off — with the full conversation history and the context that was active at that time.

The thread title and entity chips (the small tags) are generated automatically based on what was discussed. They're a quick way to find what you're looking for without searching through messages.

You can rename any thread by long-pressing on it (mobile) or clicking the title. Manual renames are permanent — Lorekeeper won't overwrite a name you chose.

---

## What "Remembering" Means

When Lorekeeper says something like "last time you mentioned your sister was going through a hard time" — that's not a fabrication. It's a retrieval from your actual record.

When it doesn't say anything about your history, it's because it genuinely doesn't have a record of that topic yet. The correct response to that is to share it.

**What Lorekeeper will never do:**
- Claim to remember something it wasn't told
- Invent prior conversations that didn't happen
- Pretend your record is richer than it is

**What Lorekeeper will do as your record grows:**
- Reference recurring people by name without being reminded who they are
- Notice when you're returning to a theme you've talked about before
- Respond to "what's been going on with work?" with actual context from your threads

---

## The Early Phase

If you're just getting started, your record is nearly empty. That's normal and expected. Don't be discouraged if responses don't reference your history yet — they can't reference what hasn't been shared.

The most useful thing to do early: share real things that are actually happening in your life. Not hypotheticals or test messages. Actual things. The record builds faster when you treat it like a real confidant rather than a demo.

---

## What Lorekeeper Doesn't Do

- It doesn't watch your behavior outside of conversations
- It doesn't import from other apps or services
- It doesn't learn from public information about you
- It doesn't share your record with anyone — your data is yours

Everything Lorekeeper knows about you comes from what you've explicitly shared in conversation.

---

## Practical Tips

**Mention names.** "My mom" eventually becomes a tracked character, but "my mom Sarah" becomes one faster and more accurately.

**Return to ongoing situations.** If something is developing over time, keep updating it in Lorekeeper. "Update on the Portland move — " is exactly the kind of message that builds continuity.

**Don't overthink categorization.** Lorekeeper classifies automatically. Just write naturally. If it misclassifies something, the worst outcome is that one message doesn't go into your record — you can always re-share it.

**Check thread entity chips.** The small tags on thread titles show who or what Lorekeeper identified as recurring in that conversation. If someone important isn't showing up as a chip, mention them more explicitly in a few messages.

**Give it time.** The first few conversations feel like any chat interface. The continuity becomes noticeable around session 5–10, when Lorekeeper starts connecting threads you didn't explicitly connect.
